Strategic Overview

The proposed venture focuses on producing heritage-variety wheat flour with organic certification, stone-milled on site, and marketed to artisanal bakeries and direct-to-consumer subscription customers. Research emphasised climate resilience, soil stewardship, and cooperative supply models suited to the Highlands and Moray regions.

Key Research Questions

  • What production methods align with both Japanese natural agriculture and UK organic standards?
  • How many hectares and what infrastructure are required for financial break-even in three years?
  • Which distribution channels in Scotland show the highest readiness for premium regenerative flour?
  • What grant, loan, or cooperative funding options de-risk the initial capital expenditure?

Methodology & Evidence Handling

Research followed a mixed-method approach combining qualitative interviews, on-site observation, and desk research. Primary information was triangulated against published academic and industry reports. Citations are managed in Zotero with DOIs where available.

Workflow

  1. Scoping: Defined hypotheses on regenerative suitability and market size.
  2. Fieldwork: Visited three Highland farms and one community mill to observe agronomy and processing.
  3. Expert validation: Conducted interviews with agronomists, soil scientists, and Japanese natural farming practitioners.
  4. Financial modelling: Built cash-flow projections under conservative, base, and stretch scenarios.

Evidence Appraisal

  • All interviews logged in Otter.ai with consent and summarised in Notion.
  • Peer-reviewed sources prioritised for agronomic data; trade publications used for price discovery.
  • Each claim in the venture memo references at least two source types (primary + secondary).

Field Visit Notes

  • Frost pockets require wind-row orientation adjustments; documented drone imagery uploaded to GIS drive.
  • Local bakery partners prefer 25kg sacks; packaging trial conducted with compostable liners.
